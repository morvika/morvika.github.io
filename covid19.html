Covid-19 Info Hub

Know your risk. Stay Informed.

Seeing first hand that my friends and family were seeing and believing misinformation about Covid-19, I decided to conduct interviews and surveys to learn how people were processing information about Covid-19 and how to how to help navigate them through the pandemic.

This led me to design a mobile app called Covid-19 Info Hub to help people understand the risks of Covid-19 and make informed decisions in their day to day lives.

Lookback

The problem

Having been hit by the Covid-19 pandemic, people have struggled in processing the vast amount information from many different sources. There are so many resources out there from news articles to social media that provide conflicting information to users and many of which provide misinformation. It has become very difficult for people to make informed decisions to help them navigate the pandemic.

The research

I conducted interviews and surveys to understand how people received their information about Covid-19 and how made day to day decisions during the pandemic. From the research, I learned what information people would find valuable and what information people were confortable to share within an app to help them make a better decision.

I learned that users were interested in learning about cases in their area and learning which activities were safe to perform.

I also learned that users wanted to a symptom checker to understand if they were likely to have contracted Covid-19; however, they were hesitant in sharing personal information. This led to designing a symptom tracker that tracked minimal information and was anonymous without any tracking information requested.

The solution

I have designed a mobile app to provide users the ability to see the prevalence of covid cases in their area, understand if they should seek medical help if they show symptoms, learn the latest news about covid-19, and understand the relative risks associated with daily activities.

Covid-19 Screening Tool

One key insight was that people could not distinguish cold and flu symptoms from covid-19 symptoms.

As a result, I developed a screening tool to help a person decide to seek medical care.

Covid-19 Risk Tool

Another key insight is people could not process the amount of conflicting information about what activities were safer than others as they were coming out of lockdowns. I build a feature to allow users to search for an activity and understand the relative risk of that activity as compared to other common activities.
